There are four major scientist that contributed to the classification of living organism.

Aristotle:

Aristotle (384-322 BCE) laid the groundwork for classification by grouping living
things into plants and animals.

Carl Linnaeus (1707-1778) revolutionized taxonomy with his binomial nomenclature system.

Charles Darwin:

Charles Darwin's (1809-1882) theory of evolution by natural selection transformed
our understanding of species relationships and he also Introduced the theory of evolution through natural selection./p>

Carl Woese:

Carl Woese (1928-2012) pioneered molecular taxonomy, proposing the three-domain system
(Bacteria, Archaea, Eukarya) based on RNA analysis
